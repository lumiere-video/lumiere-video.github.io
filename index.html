Introduction

We introduce Lumiere -- a text-to-video diffusion model designed for synthesizing videos that portray realistic, diverse and coherent motion -- a pivotal challenge in video synthesis. To this end, we introduce a model architecture that generates the entire duration of the video in one pass, followed by a spatial-only super-resolution model. Our model is based on a space-time U-Net architecture that incorporates both spatial and (importantly) temporal down- and up-sampling, and leverages a pre-trained text-to-image diffusion model. As opposed to existing methods which synthesize distant keyframes followed by temporal super-resolution, our model learns to directly generate a full-frame-rate, low resolution video by processing it in multiple space-time scales. We demonstrate state-of-the-art text-to-video generation results, as well as a range of creative applications that are easily facilitated by our design, including image-to-video, video inpainting, and stylized generation.
